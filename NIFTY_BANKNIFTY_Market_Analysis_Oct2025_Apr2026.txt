5. EXPIRY DAY BEHAVIOR (TUESDAY SHIFT)
================================================================================

---------- STRUCTURAL CHANGE ----------

Old System: Thursday weekly expiry (25-year tradition)
New System: Tuesday weekly expiry (effective Sep 1, 2025)
BSE: Thursday expiry

---------- TUESDAY vs THURSDAY EXPIRY DIFFERENCES ----------

Parameter           | Thursday (Old)         | Tuesday (New)
--------------------|------------------------|------------------------
Weekend decay       | Wed->Thu (1 day theta) | Fri->Tue (3 days theta)
                    |                        | Sat+Sun+Mon decay
Peak volatility     | Wed-Thu                | Mon-Tue
Theta acceleration  | Gradual into Thu       | Sharp Mon morning drop
Premium collapse    | Moderate               | 50-80% on expiry day
Seller advantage    | Standard               | Enhanced (3-day decay)
Buyer shock         | Moderate               | Severe Mon morning gap

---------- WEEKEND THETA DECAY (FRI -> TUE = 3 DAYS) ----------

Key dynamics:
- Option premiums soften toward end of trading week (professional
  sellers sell IV on Friday afternoon)
- Monday morning: premiums drop sharply reflecting Sat+Sun+Mon decay
- Tuesday (expiry day): remaining theta collapses 40-60% in final hours
- Net effect: Option buyers face massive erosion over the weekend

Practical data:
- If option has 10 days to expiry: ~Rs 3/day theta
- 2 days before expiry: ~Rs 10-15/day theta
- Expiry day (Tuesday): 50-80% premium collapse even without price move

---------- MAX PAIN PINNING ----------

- Max Pain = strike where option BUYERS lose the most money
- High Open Interest (OI) strikes create "pin risk"
- Price gravitates toward max pain on expiry day
- Mechanism: Market maker hedging (gamma exposure) at high OI strikes
- Studies confirm stocks tend to gravitate toward high OI strikes on expiry

---------- GAMMA EXPOSURE ON EXPIRY DAYS ----------

- ATM options: Delta can jump from 0.5 to 0.8 with just a 50-point
  Nifty move on Tuesday morning
- Option sellers face MAXIMUM gamma risk on Tuesday
- A small adverse move can wipe out entire week's premium collection
- Tuesday sees 50% higher volume and extreme volatility
- Gamma risk peaks for ATM options on expiry day

---------- TRADING STRATEGY IMPLICATIONS ----------

Day of Week  | Strategy Bias
-------------|------------------
Wednesday    | Buy premium (cheaper, less decay pressure)
Thursday     | Moderate (position building)
Friday       | Sell premium (capture weekend decay)
Monday       | Sell premium (aggressive decay, but gap risk)
Tuesday      | Extreme volatility, gamma scalping, expiry trades

KEY MODEL PARAMETERS:
- Friday close -> Monday open: expect 2-3 days of theta priced out
- Monday-Tuesday: Favor selling strategies with tight controls
- Thursday-Friday: Favor buying strategies (less aggressive decay)
- Max pain convergence: track Friday close vs max pain level
- Gamma flip point: monitor where net gamma changes sign

---------- VIX TERM STRUCTURE ----------

Note: India does not have listed VIX futures, so term structure is
implied from options at different expiries.

General framework:
- Contango (normal): Longer-dated IV > near-term IV (84% of time)
- Backwardation (stress): Near-term IV > longer-dated IV (16% of time)
- March 2026: LIKELY BACKWARDATION given VIX spike to 27+
  (near-term fear exceeding long-term expectations)

KEY MODEL PARAMETERS:
- VIX < 12: Complacency zone, consider protective puts
- VIX 13-20: Normal trading range
- VIX 20-25: Elevated risk, widen stop-losses
- VIX > 25: Capitulation zone, historical bounce probability 75%
- VIX spike > 50% in < 5 days: High probability of mean reversion
  within 4-6 weeks
